## How Owen works (project-relevant)

- **Decisive once aligned.** Once a direction is chosen, he wants
  shipping speed, not more options. Hesitation reads as wasted time.
- **Recommendation-first.** Don't waffle, don't ask "which would you
  prefer?" twice in a row. Lead with a recommended option and the main
  trade-off. He'll redirect if he disagrees.
- **Casual register.** Conversational tone; mixed-case typing; minimal
  punctuation in messages. Match his register; don't over-formalize.
- **Trusts his own taste over external first-pass reactions.** When his
  "mate" said all four logo v1 sketches were bad, Owen's gut said two
  were good — he was right and we shipped a descendant of one of them.
- **Time-boxed sessions.** Often works in evening 1–3 hour bursts.
  Wrapping up cleanly at end-of-session is more useful than starting
  a new initiative right before he stops.
- **Wants memory captured aggressively.** Default is "save more, not
  less." Both auto-memory and this brain. Said directly.
- **Wants edits batched, not sequential.** Token-cost reasoning:
  multiple edits in one turn is cheaper than back-and-forth. See
  `feedback_batch_changes.md` in auto-memory.

## How Owen talks

- "Lets..." → start now, no further design discussion needed.
- "Idk about..." → genuinely unsure; probe, don't commit.
- "Make sure..." / "Future reference..." → add to memory; this is
  durable feedback.
- Typos and dropped vowels are stylistic, not signals to clarify.
