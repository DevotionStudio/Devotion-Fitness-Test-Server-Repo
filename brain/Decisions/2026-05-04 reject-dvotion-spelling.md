# Reject "Dvotion" — keep the E

## Context

While exploring brand identity, Owen floated dropping the E from
"Devotion" → **"Dvotion"**. The motivation was real: better domain/handle
availability (devotion.* is heavily squatted), tighter visual block in
caps wordmark, less religious-iconography drag in search rankings.

## Options considered

- **Dvotion** — vowel-drop modernism (Tumblr / Flickr / Grindr era).
  Better handle availability, tighter wordmark, more SEO whitespace.
- **Devotion** (status quo) — pronounceable on first read, no
  spelling-correction friction, but heavily squatted online.

## Decision

**Keep "Devotion" — reject Dvotion.**

## Reason

Owen's own gut: "I personally feel like this is an SEO thing and sticking
with Devotion is better but I do like the name."

The trade-off:

- **Dvotion buys:** SEO discoverability, handle availability, ~5px of
  visual tightness in the wordmark.
- **Dvotion costs:** every voice / word-of-mouth / podcast / interview
  /classroom intro for the lifetime of the brand. "Spelled D-V-O-T-I-O-N,
  no E" becomes friction at every mention.

For a consumer app where word-of-mouth and voice-search matter more than
ranking #1 on Google for the literal term, the trade is bad.

The discoverability problem is solvable with a TLD modifier — and indeed
2026-05-06 we registered `devotion.fitness` and `devotionfitness.co.uk`,
which solves both the squatted-domain and the SEO disambiguation problem
at zero pronunciation cost.

## Consequences

- "Devotion" remains the working brand name.
- The midcap "DeVotion" wordmark (the V holds a barbell — see
  [V-press metaphor](../Concepts/V-press%20metaphor.md)) is the visual
  treatment that handles the "make it tighter" instinct without
  sacrificing the spelling.
- Domain modifier strategy: `.fitness` for the brand, `.co.uk` as UK
  redirect.

## Follow-ups

- Domain registration (done 2026-05-06).
- Don't resurface the Dvotion question unless Owen explicitly reopens it.
