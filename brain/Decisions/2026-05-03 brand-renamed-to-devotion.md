# Brand renamed: Anvil → Devotion

## Context

The product had been called Anvil for ~4 days, after spending 4 days as
the placeholder "GymFit". The Anvil name committed the brand to an
industrial-forge aesthetic (hammers, embers, iron) and Owen was no longer
sure that register served the long-term positioning.

## Options considered

- **Stacked** — literal-gym word; high discoverability, low brand
  distinctiveness. Competes lexically with every fitness app.
- **Anvil** (status quo) — stronger than literal but committed to a
  specific industrial aesthetic that limited the brand's posture.
- **Ratt** — short, distinctive, but odd; hard to defend the meaning.
- **Devotion** — discipline / identity word. Closer in register to
  Caliber or Future. Brand idea over feature description.

## Decision

**Devotion** is the working brand name.

## Reason

Owen explicitly preferred a brand idea over a literal description.
"Devotion" frames lifting as a daily practice (which is what the product
*does*) rather than a category (gym / fitness). The discoverability
penalty is real — people don't search "devotion app" looking for fitness —
but brand integrity compounds and discoverability problems are solvable
with marketing.

## Consequences

- All UI copy, marketing, metadata uses "Devotion".
- The Anvil-era industrial-forge visual system needs to be replaced —
  see [2026-05-03 design-pivot-liturgical-restraint](2026-05-03%20design-pivot-liturgical-restraint.md).
- The disk path stays `c:\Users\Owen\Documents\GymFit app` — don't rename
  unless explicitly asked.
- The brand name remains "working, not final" — Owen reserves the right
  to swap it again.
