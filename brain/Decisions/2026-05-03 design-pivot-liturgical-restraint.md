# Design pivot: industrial-forge → liturgical restraint

## Context

The Anvil-era visual system was committed to an industrial-forge aesthetic
— ember palette (oranges/iron), Archivo Black headlines, hammer-strike
metaphors. With the rename to "Devotion" (see
[2026-05-03 brand-renamed-to-devotion](2026-05-03%20brand-renamed-to-devotion.md)),
the industrial register no longer fit. "Devotion" reads monastic /
liturgical / disciplined, not blacksmithy.

## Options considered

- **Keep the industrial system, embrace the tension.** Hard sell — the
  name and visuals would actively fight each other. Risk: brand reads as
  confused.
- **Rebuild as fashion-magazine editorial.** Closer to publications like
  Kinfolk or Cereal. Possible but risks reading too "lifestyle aesthetic"
  without the gym substance.
- **Liturgical restraint.** Monastic visual codes — Fraunces (later
  replaced by Newsreader), bone/ink/oxblood palette, hairline rules,
  no shadows, calm hierarchy. Strength as a daily practice, framed in
  the visual language of devotional discipline.

## Decision

**Rebuild around liturgical restraint.**

## Reason

The name and visuals should reinforce each other, not fight. "Devotion"
is monastic in its connotations and the design system should match. The
liturgical-restraint frame also gives us a defensible visual difference
from every other fitness app (which all default to high-saturation,
gamified UI).

## Consequences

- Full rebuild of all four routes from scratch.
- New palette: aurora violet on lavender mist (bone / ink / oxblood,
  named for monastic ink-and-bone iconography even though the actual
  hex values are violet-coded).
- New typography: Fraunces serif + Hanken Grotesk sans (Fraunces later
  replaced by Newsreader 2026-05-06).
- Old industrial-forge component classes deleted (`.btn-ember`, etc).
- The brand-mark journey starts here: pentagon-D → vigil candle → halo
  ring → V-press, all under the liturgical-restraint umbrella.
